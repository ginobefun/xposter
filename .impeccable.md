## Design Context

### Users
Creators who write long-form posts in Markdown-first tools such as Obsidian, Typora, Notion, or local editors, then publish to X Articles. They want reliable conversion without babysitting fragile browser automation.

### Brand Personality
Focused, precise, editorial. The interface should feel like a publishing console, not a marketing page or toy utility.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light-first, quiet, high-contrast, and work-focused. Use restrained ink, paper, and signal colors; avoid neon dark dashboards, generic gradient cards, and oversized landing-page composition.

### Design Principles
- Put the publishing workflow first: draft, inspect, import, verify.
- Make state visible without noise: progress, warnings, limits, and page readiness should be clear.
- Keep controls dense but calm, with strong hierarchy and no nested cards.
- Prefer reversible actions and clear recovery when X changes or an import fails.
- Design for repeated daily use rather than a one-time onboarding moment.
